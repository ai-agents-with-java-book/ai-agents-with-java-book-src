NovaTech Global Holdings Inc.

2024 Annual Financial and Operational Report
Fiscal Year Ended: December 31, 2024

Executive Summary

Fiscal year 2024 marked another year of record performance for NovaTech Global Holdings Inc., a multinational technology company headquartered in Singapore with operations spanning over 60 countries. NovaTech develops and markets cloud computing infrastructure, artificial intelligence systems, enterprise software, and consumer electronics under the NovaTech brand ecosystem.

Despite economic headwinds and persistent component supply volatility, NovaTech achieved USD 184.2 billion in total revenue, up 9.6% year-over-year, and a net income of USD 41.7 billion, representing an 11.8% increase compared to 2023. Growth was driven by the company’s NovaCloud™ and NovaAI™ product lines, supported by sustained demand for connected devices and subscription-based digital services.

NovaTech’s ongoing transformation toward a cloud-first, AI-driven model continues to deliver strong returns and operational resilience.

Segment Overview
1. Cloud & AI Services

Revenue: USD 76.5 billion (up 13.7%)

Operating Margin: 38.2%

The Cloud & AI Services division remains NovaTech’s largest revenue generator. NovaCloud™ maintained its position among the top three global IaaS providers, while the NovaAI™ Platform saw 22% growth in enterprise adoption.

New enterprise clients include HSBC, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, and NHS Digital UK. AI model training workloads tripled year-over-year, supported by the rollout of NovaCore X3 processors optimized for generative AI.

AI-driven operational tools reduced data center energy usage by 9% per compute cycle, improving efficiency and environmental sustainability.

2. Consumer Devices & Electronics

Revenue: USD 61.8 billion (up 6.1%)

Operating Margin: 24.5%

Consumer demand remained resilient despite inflationary pressures. The NovaPhone X5 achieved record sales with integrated AI-powered imaging and health-tracking features.

NovaTech’s NovaVision OLED Pro displays continued to capture market share in the premium segment. The NovaHome ecosystem — connecting smart devices, appliances, and entertainment systems — saw a 29% increase in active users, strengthening recurring hardware-linked revenue streams.

3. Software & Digital Platforms

Revenue: USD 45.9 billion (up 8.9%)

Operating Margin: 40.3%

Software and digital platform revenue benefited from growth in the NovaOffice™, NovaPlay™, and NovaAI Suite products.
Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) from software subscriptions reached USD 18.7 billion, up 15% year-over-year.

The company expanded its enterprise solutions portfolio with NovaWork™, an integrated productivity suite with AI-assisted document creation and communication tools.

Management Discussion & Analysis (MD&A)
Revenue Growth and Profitability

NovaTech’s 9.6% revenue increase was primarily driven by cloud adoption and subscription expansion. The company’s strategic pivot toward services and recurring revenue lifted gross margins to 56.4% and operating margins to 31.4%.

While hardware sales remain cyclical, service-based income now represents 57% of total revenue, up from 48% in 2021 — signaling greater business stability.

Cost Structure and Efficiency

The company achieved a 4% reduction in cost of goods sold (COGS) due to automation and improved logistics. Over 80% of NovaTech’s manufacturing partners now operate under its Integrated Smart Supply Program (ISSP), enhancing transparency and traceability.

Cash Flow and Capital Allocation

Free cash flow grew 12.7% to USD 45.2 billion, primarily driven by higher subscription income and lower inventory levels.
NovaTech deployed USD 10.4 billion toward share repurchases and USD 6.3 billion in dividends, while maintaining a cash balance of USD 72.1 billion as of year-end.

Acquisitions and Partnerships

The USD 4.3 billion acquisition of Helion Systems strengthened NovaTech’s semiconductor capabilities.
Strategic alliances with AccuData, Tata Communications, and Siemens Digital Industries broadened NovaTech’s AI applications in industrial automation and enterprise solutions.

Market Trends

Management identifies four key long-term growth drivers:

Enterprise AI adoption across all industries.

Cloud-native application migration accelerated by regulatory compliance needs.

Integration of AI into consumer electronics, creating new user experiences.

Sustainability and energy-efficient computing as competitive differentiators.

Operational Outlook

For 2025, NovaTech targets 7–10% revenue growth and expects gross margin improvement of 50–70 basis points, supported by scale efficiencies and new AI-driven offerings.
R&D will remain above 11% of revenue, with an emphasis on edge AI, quantum computing, and data privacy technologies.

Risk Factors
1. Market Competition

NovaTech operates in highly competitive markets that include major global technology companies. Price pressure, rapid innovation cycles, and aggressive new entrants could erode market share, particularly in cloud services and consumer electronics.

2. Supply Chain and Component Shortages

While the company has diversified its supplier base, it remains dependent on semiconductor manufacturers. Global supply chain disruptions or geopolitical tensions could affect production and delivery timelines.

3. Cybersecurity and Data Privacy

As NovaTech processes increasing volumes of user and enterprise data, cyberattacks pose a significant risk. A major security breach could result in regulatory penalties, reputational damage, and financial losses.

4. Regulatory and Geopolitical Risks

The company faces complex and evolving regulatory environments across jurisdictions — especially regarding data sovereignty, antitrust laws, and AI ethics. Unfavorable policy changes could impact global operations or increase compliance costs.

5. Macroeconomic Factors

Fluctuations in currency exchange rates, inflation, and interest rates can impact revenue and capital expenditures. Demand for discretionary electronics may decline during economic downturns.

6. Technological Disruption

Rapid innovation in generative AI, quantum computing, and edge processing presents both opportunity and risk. Competitors introducing disruptive architectures could outpace NovaTech’s technology roadmap.

7. Environmental and Social Risks

Failure to meet sustainability targets or ESG expectations could lead to negative investor sentiment and reputational harm, especially given NovaTech’s high public visibility.

Sustainability and ESG Performance

NovaTech’s commitment to sustainability extends beyond compliance. In 2024:

72% renewable energy use globally.

Carbon neutrality achieved across direct operations in North America.

NovaCircular™ program recycled 34,000 metric tons of electronic waste.

Published the 2024 AI Ethics Framework, ensuring transparency and accountability in AI model development.

Employee engagement scores reached 88%, and workforce diversity improved, with women representing 43% of leadership roles globally.

2025 Strategic Priorities

Expand NovaAI™ into 20 additional enterprise markets and launch AI copilots in all productivity applications.

Deploy 10 new data centers in Africa and Latin America to strengthen low-latency AI infrastructure.

Launch NovaPhone X6 with embedded AI inference chips and advanced health monitoring.

Achieve 80% renewable energy usage and reduce total emissions by 30% vs. 2020 baseline.

Notes to the Financial Statements

The following notes provide supplementary information and explanations relevant to the consolidated financial statements of NovaTech Global Holdings Inc. and its subsidiaries (“the Group”) for the year ended December 31, 2024.

Note 1: Basis of Presentation

The consolidated financial statements have been prepared in accordance with International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). All intercompany transactions and balances have been eliminated in consolidation. The fiscal year of the Group ends on December 31 of each year.

The presentation currency is United States dollars (USD), and all amounts are expressed in billions unless otherwise stated.

Note 2: Summary of Significant Accounting Policies

Revenue Recognition: Revenue is recognized upon transfer of control to customers, primarily under IFRS 15. Subscription revenue is recognized over the term of service contracts.

R&D Costs: Research costs are expensed as incurred, while development costs are capitalized when technical feasibility and probable future benefits are established.

Foreign Currency Translation: Assets and liabilities of foreign subsidiaries are translated using the closing rate; income and expenses are translated using average rates during the year.

Lease Accounting: The Group applies IFRS 16. Right-of-use assets and corresponding lease liabilities are recognized for long-term property and data center leases.
